## Rulings (each = the audit's recommended option, chosen by the principal)

1. **IL1-A — same-window responses count; the 100 anchor is reachable.**
   The emergence changeset itself is scanned for responsive edits, so a
   mitigation landing in the window the float turned negative reads
   **latency 0** and the published "0 updates → 100" anchor fires.
   Previously the scan started at the next changeset: the minimum observable
   latency was 1 (ceiling 85), and the audit's probe showed a duration
   halved in the emergence window scoring **20 ("did not act")** while the
   identical edit a month later scored 85 — the fastest responder got the
   worst grade.  Standing disclosure: causality is asserted by ADJACENCY,
   not sequence — a same-window edit is observationally simultaneous with
   the emergence (in kind, true at every latency here).
2. **IL2-A — KM right-censoring; ignored chains reach the score.**
   The headline median is a Kaplan-Meier estimate over ALL events: resolved
   events are deaths at their latency; never-resolved chains are
   right-censored at their follow-up (largest observable latency,
   `ILEvent.censored_at_updates`).  Scoring (`_li08_score`):
   - KM median reached → piecewise curve on the median.
   - KM median NOT reached (the majority of chains went unresolved through
     follow-up) → the `unresolved_only_score` (20) "did-not-act" branch,
     with the follow-up lower bound reported as the value.  This extends the
     existing yaml semantic ("nothing ever resolved → 20") to
     majority-unresolved records — the audit's probe (1 chain answered in 1
     update + 5 ignored forever) moves 85 → **20**, no longer identical to a
     perfect responder.
   - No latency information at all (no events, or every event censored at
     zero follow-up) → ungradeable N/A, never 20.
3. **IL3 — subsumed by IL2-A.**  A final-window emergence has exactly its
   own window of opportunity; unresolved there, it is censored at latency 0
   and carries no information: the audit's probe (2-schedule series, sole
   final-window emergence) moves 20 → **N/A**.
4. **IL4a/IL4b — population.**  LOE/WBS-summary/hammock activities are
   excluded from the emergence set (a hammock's float derives from the work
   it spans; the spanned work's chain carries the emergence — counting the
   hammock double-counts, and a hammock-only chain drove LI-08 to 20 in the
   audit probe).  Activities **completed in the later file** are excluded (a
   finished activity's float is not a mitigable problem; inclusion was
   exporter-dependent).
5. **IL5 — the L9 convention for day figures.**  Missing or non-increasing
   data dates withhold `il_days`/`median_il_days` with a disclosure (never a
   negative day count); the scored update-unit latency is unaffected.
6. **IL6 — standing disclosures** on every `ILResult`: response definition
   (any touching edit — responsiveness, not effectiveness), the adjacency
   caveat, KM censoring semantics, population and emergence definitions
   (born-negative activities are not emergences), chain construction, the
   duration-string format coupling, and the day-figure basis.
