## What was ported (all 12 rulings + the peer-review close-out, as ruled)

Scoring (`scorecard.py::_li02_score`, scorecard.yaml LI-02):

- **L1 (defect):** the not-reached branch grants 100 when fewer than 10% of
  relationships DIED (`deaths_pass_threshold`, renamed from
  `censored_pass_threshold`).  Previously it tested the CENSORED fraction —
  inverted vs its own published rationale; a frozen network scored 70 and
  the 100 branch was unreachable.
- **L10 (defect):** a median with no usable months basis is UNGRADEABLE
  (None), never scored by the censoring branch.  Previously, a 19/20-die
  series with missing data dates scored 100 via the L1 inversion (a 0 ↔ 100
  swing on data-date availability alone — reproduced on this base in the
  audit's LHL-2 probe).
- Offender count = relationship deaths; not-reached scores report the
  longest-follow-up lower bound as the value.

Metric (`analytics/li_record.py`):

- **L5+L7:** lifespans measured in CALENDAR DAYS between data dates (KM in
  days; months = days/30.44), deaths dated at the MIDPOINT of the
  disappearance window.  Replaces update-count KM × series-mean cadence and
  last-seen death dating (which made every first-window deletion a
  0-duration event).
- **L2:** when the KM curve never crosses 0.5, the reported median is the
  LONGEST OBSERVED FOLLOW-UP (flagged lower bound), not the last event time
  (the demo card read "0.0 months"; it now reads "at least 3.0 months").
  The on/off ratio is suppressed unless both medians were actually reached.
- **L4a:** ties with an LOE/WBS-summary/hammock endpoint are excluded.
- **L4b:** ties are censored at the update both endpoints complete; ties
  born on already-completed work are not observed (immortal completed-work
  logic no longer inflates survival).
- **L9:** missing or non-increasing data dates withhold the months basis
  with an explicit disclosure (never a negative or silent-None figure; the
  audit's LHL-5 probe on this base reported −0.97 months).
- **L8:** on/off-path membership is "on the driving path at ANY update
  while alive" (was: at birth only); unclassifiable instances are counted
  (`split_dropped`) and disclosed; ratio direction (>1 = driving logic more
  stable) documented in output.  Includes the v0.4.5 post-implementation
  peer-review fix: the completion-censor update is recorded as the
  instance's last-alive update, so a tie that becomes driving in the same
  update both endpoints complete classifies ON-path.
- **L3 (affirmed):** signatures stay CODE-keyed — consistent with the
  code-keyed logic diff (TRD-03) and robust to UID churn on re-export —
  with the re-code consequence disclosed.
- **L6:** `LHLResult.first_pair_excluded` reports the EFFECTIVE exclusion
  state (with exactly 2 schedules the requested exclusion cannot apply and
  is disclosed).
- **X1+X2:** standing `disclosures` list on every result (signature
  definition incl. the duplicate-tie collapse, population rules, units
  basis, censoring %, split basis); the LI-02 wiring narrative rounds the
  months, always carries censoring, and surfaces the disclosures as
  findings.
