# LI-03 FRB — Wave-2 governed revision (rulings FR2-A / FR3-A / FR4)

**Status:** accepted · **Date:** 2026-07-12 · **Principal:** Alex Bachowski

was fixed earlier in Wave 0 (CHANGELOG "LI wiring defect batch").

## Rulings

1. **FR2-A — overdue bucket.**  Forecast horizons ≤ 0 (an unfinished
   activity whose forecast finish is on or before the data date — exactly
   where forecast credibility dies) get their own `overdue(<=0d)` bucket,
   reported and scored like the others.  Previously they fell out of every
   bucket, biasing every band toward the well-behaved part of the record
   and making `len(observations) ≠ Σ bucket n` (audit probe: 3 observations,
   1 bucketed; now 3/3).  `frb_apply_forward` bands overdue horizons the
   same way (subject to its existing n ≥ 5 floor).
2. **FR3-A — n ≥ 5 scoring floor.**  The scored band (largest bucket's
   P90−P10 width) is gradeable only when that bucket holds at least 5
   resolved forecasts — the same reliability floor `frb_apply_forward`
   already applied to live banding.  Below it, LI-03 is **NOT EVALUATED**
   with a reason through the wiring (insufficient basis ⇒ ungraded, not
   free credit — the ruled L10/sentinel convention).  A single resolved
   forecast can no longer certify a forecaster at 100.
3. **FR4 — standing disclosures** on every `FRBResult`: the observation
   model (per-update re-forecasts of one activity are AUTOCORRELATED
   observations resolved by the same actual), the fixed Mon–Fri 8 h error
   calendar, the horizon basis and bucket boundaries incl. the overdue
   rule, code-keyed actual matching (a re-coded activity's forecast never
   resolves), and the scored basis with its floor.

## Note for Report Card users

Series whose largest bucket held fewer than 5 resolved forecasts previously
scored 100 by construction (width 0 on a thin sample, or the FR1 constant);
they now report NOT EVALUATED and the Forecast Credibility category
reweights accordingly.  This is the intended correction, not drift.
